THE RISE OF NAZI GERMANY AND WORLD WAR II (1919-1945)
======================================================

1. POST-WWI GERMANY (1919-1933)
--------------------------------

1.1 Treaty of Versailles (1919)
- Territorial Losses:
  * 13% territory
  * 10% population (6.5 million)
  * All colonies (2.3 million km²)
- Military Restrictions:
  * 100,000 army max
  * No tanks/aircraft/submarines
  * Rhineland demilitarized
- Reparations:
  * 132 billion gold marks (≈$442 billion today)
  * Paid until 2010 (final payment)

1.2 Weimar Republic (1919-1933)
- Economic Crises:
  * 1923 Hyperinflation:
    - Nov 1923: 4.2 trillion marks = $1
    - Middle class savings wiped out
  * 1929 Great Depression:
    - 33% unemployment (6 million)
    - Industrial production ↓ 40%
- Political Instability:
  * 1919-1933: 20 different cabinets
  * 1920s: 376 political murders (354 by right-wing)

2. NAZI RISE TO POWER (1920-1933)
----------------------------------

2.1 Early Nazi Party (1920-1923)
- 1920: DAP becomes NSDAP (Nazi Party)
- 25-Point Program:
  * Revise Versailles
  * Remove Jews from citizenship
  * Lebensraum (living space)
- 1923 Beer Hall Putsch:
  * Failed Munich coup
  * Hitler imprisoned (writes Mein Kampf)

2.2 Legal Path to Power (1924-1933)
- Electoral Growth:
  * 1928: 2.6% (12 seats)
  * 1930: 18.3% (107 seats)
  * Jul 1932: 37.3% (230 seats)
- Backroom Deals:
  * 30 Jan 1933: Hitler appointed Chancellor
  * Conservative elites' miscalculation

3. NAZI GERMANY (1933-1939)
----------------------------

3.1 Consolidation of Power (1933-1934)
- Reichstag Fire (27 Feb 1933):
  * Emergency Decree suspends civil rights
- Enabling Act (23 Mar 1933):
  * Dictatorial powers to Hitler
- Night of Long Knives (Jun 1934):
  * 85 SA leaders executed
  * Army swears oath to Hitler

3.2 Nazi Policies
- Economic:
  * 1933-1936: Unemployment ↓ 6m → 1.5m
  * Mefo bills (hidden rearmament financing)
- Social Control:
  * Gestapo (30,000 agents by 1939)
  * 1933-1945: 77,000 executed for resistance
- Persecution:
  * 1935 Nuremberg Laws
  * 1938 Kristallnacht (91 killed, 30,000 arrested)

3.3 Foreign Policy (1933-1939)
- Violating Versailles:
  * 1935: Conscription reintroduced
  * 1936: Rhineland remilitarized
- Expansion:
  * 1938: Anschluss with Austria
  * 1938: Sudetenland (Munich Agreement)
  * Mar 1939: Czechoslovakia occupied
- Alliances:
  * 1936 Rome-Berlin Axis
  * 1939 Pact of Steel with Italy
  * Aug 1939 Nazi-Soviet Pact

4. WORLD WAR II (1939-1945)
---------------------------

4.1 European Theater
- Blitzkrieg (1939-1941):
  * Poland (Sep 1939): 36 days
  * France (May-Jun 1940): 46 days
- Key Battles:
  * Britain (1940-41): 23,000 civilian deaths
  * Stalingrad (1942-43): 2 million casualties
  * Normandy (1944): 156,000 Allied troops landed

4.2 Holocaust (1941-1945)
- Death Toll:
  * 6 million Jews
  * 11 million total victims
- Methods:
  * Einsatzgruppen (1.3 million shot)
  * 6 extermination camps (3 million gassed)
  * 42,500 camps/ghettos total

4.3 Pacific Theater
- Pearl Harbor (7 Dec 1941):
  * 2,403 Americans killed
  * 8 battleships damaged/sunk
- Island Hopping:
  * Guadalcanal (1942-43): 7,100 US dead
  * Iwo Jima (1945): 6,800 US dead

5. GERMAN DEFEAT (1943-1945)
-----------------------------

5.1 Turning Points
- 1942:
  * El Alamein (Oct)
  * Stalingrad (Nov)
- 1944:
  * D-Day (Jun)
  * Operation Bagration (Soviet advance)

5.2 Final Days
- Battle of Berlin (Apr-May 1945):
  * 80,000 Soviet dead
  * 30 Apr: Hitler suicide
- Surrender:
  * 7 May: German surrender
  * 8 May: V-E Day
