THE NUCLEAR AGE AND THE COLD WAR (1945-1991)
=============================================

1. ORIGINS OF THE COLD WAR (1945-1947)
---------------------------------------

1.1 Post-WWII Division of Europe
- Yalta Conference (Feb 1945):
  * "Spheres of influence" agreed
  * Stalin promised free elections (not implemented)
- Potsdam Conference (Jul-Aug 1945):
  * Germany divided into 4 zones
  * Reparations plan established
- Percentages Agreement (Oct 1944):
  * Churchill/Stalin division of Balkans

1.2 Early Tensions
- 1946:
  * Churchill's "Iron Curtain" speech (Mar)
  * Greek Civil War (Communists vs Monarchists)
- 1947:
  * Truman Doctrine ($400M to Greece/Turkey)
  * Marshall Plan ($13B for European recovery)

2. NUCLEAR ARMS RACE (1945-1962)
---------------------------------

2.1 Key Developments
- U.S. Nuclear Monopoly:
  * 1945: Trinity Test (Jul 16)
  * 1945: Hiroshima (Aug 6, 70-80k killed)
  * 1945: Nagasaki (Aug 9, 40-75k killed)
- Soviet Achievements:
  * 1949: First Soviet test (Joe-1)
  * 1953: Thermonuclear weapon (400kt)

2.2 Delivery Systems
- Bombers:
  * U.S. B-52 (1955, 8,800km range)
  * Soviet Tu-95 (1956, 12,800km range)
- Missiles:
  * 1957: ICBMs developed (Atlas, R-7)
  * 1960: SLBMs (Polaris, 4,600km range)

3. MAJOR CRISES (1948-1962)
----------------------------

3.1 Berlin Blockade (1948-1949)
- Soviet land blockade (Jun 1948)
- Allied airlift:
  * 278,228 flights
  * 2.3 million tons supplied
- Results:
  * NATO formed (Apr 1949)
  * Germany formally divided (1949)

3.2 Korean War (1950-1953)
- Casualties:
  * 36,914 US dead
  * 215,000 North Korean dead
  * 400,000 Chinese dead
- Armistice:
  * 38th parallel maintained
  * DMZ established

3.3 Cuban Missile Crisis (Oct 1962)
- 13-day confrontation
- Soviet missiles in Cuba:
  * 42 launchers
  * 36 nuclear warheads
- Resolution:
  * US missiles removed from Turkey
  * Hotline established

4.1 Arms Control Agreements
- 1963: Limited Test Ban Treaty
- 1968: Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty
- 1972: SALT I (limits on launchers)

4.2 Proxy Wars
- Vietnam War (1955-1975):
  * 58,220 US dead
  * 1.1 million NVA/VC dead

  * Cuba sent 50,000 troops
  * US/South Africa supported UNITA

5. SECOND COLD WAR (1979-1985)
-------------------------------

5.1 Renewed Tensions
- 1979: Soviet invasion of Afghanistan
  * 15,000 Soviet dead
  * US spent $3B supporting mujahideen
- 1983:
  * Able Archer war scare
  * SDI ("Star Wars") announced

6. END OF THE COLD WAR (1985-1991)
-----------------------------------

6.1 Gorbachev Reforms
- Glasnost (openness)
- Perestroika (restructuring)
- 1987: INF Treaty (eliminated medium-range missiles)

6.2 Collapse of Eastern Bloc
- 1989:
  * Poland: Solidarity wins elections (Jun)
  * Hungary: Opens border (May)
  * Berlin Wall falls (Nov 9)
- 1991:
  * Warsaw Pact dissolved (Jul)
  * USSR dissolved (Dec 25)

7. NUCLEAR ARSENALS (PEAK LEVELS)
----------------------------------
- United States:
  * 31,255 warheads (1967)
  * Delivery systems: 2,468
- Soviet Union:
  * 40,159 warheads (1986)
  * Delivery systems: 2,500+

8. HUMAN COST OF COLD WAR
-------------------------
- Proxy wars: 20 million dead
- Nuclear testing:
  * 528 atmospheric tests
  * Estimated 2.4 million cancer cases
- Political repression:
  * Gulag system: 18 million prisoners
  * McCarthyism: 10,000+ lost jobs
