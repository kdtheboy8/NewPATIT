THE WORLD IN THE 15TH AND 16TH CENTURIES: A COMPREHENSIVE CHRONOLOGY
==================================================================

1. GLOBAL EXPLORATION (1405-1600)
---------------------------------

1.1 Chinese Maritime Expeditions (1405-1433)
- Zheng He's Fleet:
  * 7 expeditions
  * Largest fleet: 317 ships (62 treasure ships)
  * Crew: 27,000+ men
  * Dimensions of flagship: 135m long × 55m wide
- Key Voyages:
  * 1405-07: Champa → Java → Malacca → Ceylon → Calicut
  * 1413-15: Reached Hormuz, Aden, and East Africa
  * 1421-23: Possibly rounded Cape of Good Hope

1.2 European Exploration
- Portuguese Breakthroughs:
  * 1419: Madeira discovered (João Gonçalves Zarco)
  * 1488: Bartolomeu Dias rounds Cape (34°21'S)
  * 1498: Vasco da Gama reaches Calicut (May 20)
- Spanish Voyages:
  * 1492: Columbus' fleet (Santa María 18m × 6m)
    - October 12: Landfall at San Salvador (2:00 AM)
    - 90 crew across 3 ships
  * 1519-22: Magellan-Elcano circumnavigation
    - 5 ships, 237 men
    - Only 18 complete journey

2. MAJOR EMPIRES (1400-1600)
-----------------------------

2.1 Ming Dynasty China (1368-1644)
- Population:
  * 1400: 65 million
  * 1600: 150 million
- Grand Canal Expansion:
  * 1411-1415: 2,000km rebuilt
  * Capacity: 330,000 tons/year
- Forbidden City:
  * Construction: 1406-1420
  * 980 buildings, 720,000m²

2.2 Ottoman Expansion
- Key Conquests:
  * 1453: Constantinople (April 6-May 29)
    - 80,000 Ottomans vs 7,000 defenders
    - 55-day siege
  * 1517: Mamluk Sultanate falls
    - January 22: Cairo captured
- Suleiman's Reign (1520-1566):
  * 13 major campaigns
  * 1529: First Vienna siege (120,000 troops)

2.3 Aztec Empire (1428-1521)
- Triple Alliance:
  * Tenochtitlan population: 200,000+
  * Templo Mayor: 60m high
  * 1487: 4,000 sacrificed in 4 days
- Spanish Conquest:
  * 1519: Cortés lands (11 ships, 500 men)
  * 1521: Siege of Tenochtitlan (75 days)

3. SCIENTIFIC REVOLUTION (1450-1600)
------------------------------------

3.1 Printing Revolution
- Gutenberg Press (1450):
  * 180 bibles printed (1454-55)
  * 42-line format
- Spread:
  * 1476: Caxton in England
  * By 1500: 236 European print shops

3.2 Astronomical Discoveries
- Copernicus:
  * 1543: "De Revolutionibus" published
  * Heliocentric model calculations
- Tycho Brahe:
  * 1572: Supernova observed (November 11)
  * Uraniborg observatory (1576-1597)

4. RELIGIOUS REFORMATIONS (1517-1600)
-------------------------------------

4.1 Protestant Reformation
- October 31, 1517:
  * Luther's 95 Theses nailed to Wittenberg door
  * Printed copies spread in 2 months
- Key Texts:
  * 1522: German New Testament (3,000 copies)
  * 1536: Calvin's "Institutes" (Latin edition)

4.2 Catholic Counter-Reformation
- Council of Trent (1545-1563):
  * 25 sessions
  * 270+ decrees
- Jesuit Order:
  * 1540: Approved by Pope Paul III
  * 1556: 1,000 members

5. ECONOMIC TRANSFORMATIONS
---------------------------

5.1 Price Revolution (1470-1620)
- Price Increases:
  * Grain: 400% rise
  * Land rents: 500% increase
- Causes:
  * New World silver (1545 Potosí mines)
    - 25,000 tons silver extracted (1545-1600)
    * 1565: Manila galleon trade begins

5.2 Slave Trade Expansion
- Transatlantic Trade:
  * 1526: First direct shipment (São Tomé → Caribbean)
  * 1562: Hawkins' first voyage (300 slaves)
- African Kingdoms:
  * Kongo converts 1491
  * Benin brassworks peak (1480-1550)

6. MILITARY INNOVATIONS
-----------------------

6.1 Gunpowder Revolution
- Artillery:
  * 1453: Ottoman bombards (8.2m long, 75cm bore)
  * 1494: French siege guns (60lb shot)
- Small Arms:
  * 1521: Arquebus effective range (100m)
  * 1543: Muskets introduced to Japan (Tanegashima)

6.2 Naval Warfare
- Carrack Design:
  * Santa María: 150 tons
  * Grace Dieu (1418): 1,400 tons
- Galleon Development:
  * 1550s: 500-ton standard
  * 1588: English race-built galleons (34 guns)
